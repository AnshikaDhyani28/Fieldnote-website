About us

We treat learning like fieldwork, not a syllabus.

"You don't learn a trade by memorising it. You learn it by doing it badly, then a little less badly." — Fieldnote founding note, 2022

Fieldnote started with a simple complaint: most online courses hand out certificates for watching videos, not for building anything real. So we built the opposite — a place where every learner keeps an actual log of what they shipped, who reviewed it, and what they'd do differently next time.

Today we run twelve-week programs across web development, data literacy, product design, and technical writing, each led by a working practitioner who mentors a small cohort instead of lecturing a crowd. No pre-recorded courses, no vanity certificates — just projects, feedback, and a log you can actually show an employer.

How it works

Three steps, in order, every cohort.

01

Apply and get matched

Tell us what you want to build. We place you in a cohort with a mentor who works in that field today.

02

Ship weekly, get reviewed

Every week ends with a real deliverable and written feedback from your mentor — logged, not lost in a forum.

03

Graduate with a log, not just a certificate

Leave with a public record of everything you built, reviewed and revised — something you can actually show.
